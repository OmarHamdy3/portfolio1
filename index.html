POWER BI · RETAIL ANALYTICS

Superstore Business Analysis

An end-to-end sales and profitability dashboard built on the classic Superstore dataset, designed to surface exactly where a retail business is winning and where it's quietly losing money.

ROLE
Data Analyst
DURATION
3 weeks
TOOLS
Power BI, DAX, Excel
FOCUS
Sales & Profitability

01 Project Overview

Brief Project Idea: A comprehensive business intelligence dashboard for a retail superstore, providing a 360-degree view of sales, profit, and customer performance.

Main Objective: To identify underperforming product categories and regions, and uncover the drivers of profitability to guide strategic decisions.

Data Type: Structured transactional data (orders, returns, customers, products).

Key Metrics Tracked: Total Sales, Total Profit, Profit Margin, Sales Growth (YoY), Discount Impact, Average Order Value, Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

02 Business Problem

Leadership could see overall revenue was growing, but they lacked visibility into which specific segments (products, regions, customer types) were actually profitable. The finance team suspected that heavy discounting was eroding margins in certain categories, but the data wasn't readily available in a format that allowed for quick analysis. Decision-makers were operating on intuition rather than data, leading to potentially inefficient marketing spend and inventory allocation.

03 Analysis Objectives

  • Build a single source of truth for sales, profit, and customer data.
  • Identify the top 5 most and least profitable product sub-categories.
  • Quantify the impact of discounts on overall profit margin.
  • Analyze regional performance to find underperforming markets.
  • Create an interactive tool for leadership to explore the data on their own.

04 Dataset Description

Key Columns: Order ID, Order Date, Ship Date, Customer ID, Product ID, Category, Sub-Category, Sales, Quantity, Discount, Profit, Region, State, City.

Data Quality Issues Identified: Missing values in the 'Postal Code' column (handled by imputation), inconsistent date formats, duplicate order-line items (removed), and negative profit values that required investigation.

05 The Solution: Unified Analytics Engine

A multi-page Power BI dashboard was developed, structured as follows:

  • Executive Summary: High-level KPIs and trends for quick performance snapshots.
  • Profit & Loss Analysis: Deep dive into profitability by product, region, and segment.
  • Customer Insights: Analysis of customer segments and their buying behavior.
  • Geographic View: Interactive map highlighting sales and profit by state.

06 Data Cleaning & Preparation

Data preparation was performed in Power Query Editor:

  • Handling Missing Values: Postal Code blanks were filled using a fallback method based on City/State.
  • Standardization: All date columns were converted to a consistent format.
  • Data Type Enforcement: Ensured all numeric fields (Sales, Profit, Quantity) were correctly typed.
  • Data Modeling: A star schema was built with a central Sales fact table and dimension tables for Date, Customer, Product, and Location.

07 Exploratory Data Analysis

Key findings from the initial exploration in Power BI:

  • Sales Trend: Year-over-year sales were growing steadily, but profit margins were declining.
  • Discount Sensitivity: High discounts in certain sub-categories (e.g., Furniture) were not generating enough additional sales volume to offset the profit loss.
  • Regional Performance: The Central region had the highest sales but the lowest profit margin.

08 Key Business Insights

  • Insight 1: Three product sub-categories (Tables, Bookcases, and Supplies) were consistently unprofitable despite high sales volume.
  • Insight 2: Discounts exceeding 40% rarely resulted in profitable transactions.
  • Insight 3: The South region showed the strongest profit margin, suggesting a more effective pricing strategy there.
  • Insight 4: Corporate customers had the highest Lifetime Value (LTV), making them a key target segment for retention.

09 Project Demo Video

A walkthrough of the dashboard and its key features is available on request.

Video Chapters:
00:00 — Introduction & Executive Summary
01:30 — Profit & Loss Deep Dive
03:45 — Geographic Performance Analysis
05:20 — Customer Insights & Segmentation

10 Interactive Dashboards

The final solution includes an interactive Power BI report with:

  • Cross-filtering across all visualizations (click on a region to filter the entire page).
  • Drill-through capabilities from Region → State → City.
  • Bookmarks to switch between high-level and detailed views.
  • Slicers for date ranges, product categories, and customer segments.

11 Strategic Recommendations

  • Recommendation 1: Reduce or eliminate discounts on the three unprofitable sub-categories (Tables, Bookcases, Supplies) and focus on bundling them with profitable items.
  • Recommendation 2: Implement a "minimum price" rule to prevent discounts above 35% without manager approval.
  • Recommendation 3: Investigate the profitability drivers in the South region and apply similar strategies to the Central and East regions.
  • Recommendation 4: Develop a loyalty program specifically for Corporate customers to increase retention and LTV.

12 Tools & Technologies

Primary: Microsoft Power BI (DAX, Power Query).

Secondary: Microsoft Excel (for initial data sanity checks).

Methodology: Star Schema Data Modeling, Agile Dashboard Design.

13 Estimated Business Impact

By acting on the insights provided by this dashboard, the business could potentially:

  • Increase overall profit margin by 3-5% by optimizing discounting practices.
  • Reduce inventory holding costs for unprofitable items.
  • Improve marketing ROI by focusing on high-value customer segments.
  • Save 10-15 hours per week previously spent on manual reporting and data reconciliation.

14 Overall Business Impact Summary

This project transformed the way the Superstore leadership team understood its business. By centralizing data and providing clear, actionable insights, the dashboard enabled a shift from intuition-based to data-driven decision-making, directly impacting the company's bottom line.

15 Analysis Summary

The Superstore Business Analysis project successfully answered the core business questions about profitability and performance. The final deliverable was a reliable, user-friendly dashboard that empowers stakeholders to make faster and more confident decisions about pricing, marketing, and inventory strategy.

Road Accident Intelligence Platform

An investigative analysis of road accident records, built to answer a very practical question: where and when do accidents actually happen, and what conditions make them more likely?

ROLE
Data Analyst
DURATION
4 weeks
TOOLS
SQL Server, Python, Power BI
FOCUS
Risk Hotspots

01 Project Overview

Brief Project Idea: A data-driven platform to analyze road accident data, identifying high-risk locations, weather conditions, and time patterns.

Main Objective: To provide safety teams with actionable intelligence to reduce accident severity and frequency.

Data Type: Structured historical accident records, weather data, and road infrastructure data.

Key Metrics Tracked: Accident Frequency, Severity Index, Weather Impact Score, Time of Day Risk, Location Risk Scores.

02 Business Problem

Raw accident records were inconsistent — location data was messy, weather conditions were often omitted, and there was no easy way to correlate accidents across multiple dimensions. City planners and safety officers had no clear way to prioritize interventions or allocate resources effectively.

03 Analysis Objectives

  • Clean and standardize accident records from multiple sources.
  • Identify the top 10 high-risk accident locations (by severity and frequency).
  • Analyze the correlation between weather conditions and accident severity.
  • Determine peak times for accidents (by hour, day, season).
  • Create an interactive dashboard for safety teams to explore data on their own.

04 Dataset Description

Key Columns: Accident ID, Date, Time, Location (Lat/Long, City, Road Type), Weather Conditions (Temperature, Visibility, Precipitation), Severity (Fatal, Serious, Slight), Number of Vehicles Involved.

Data Quality Issues Identified: Missing location coordinates (geocoded using City/Road info), inconsistent severity categories (mapped to a 3-level scale), incomplete weather data (backfilled using a weather API).

05 The Solution

A centralized analytics platform with:

  • Data Pipeline: SQL Server for data storage and transformation, Python for ETL and advanced analysis.
  • Analytics Engine: Power BI for interactive visualization, with a focus on geographic mapping and time-series analysis.
  • Reporting: Automated weekly reports highlighting emerging hotspots.

06 Data Cleaning & Preparation

  • Geocoding: Used Python libraries to convert City/Road information to coordinates.
  • Weather Data Integration: Used an external API to fill missing weather data.
  • Severity Mapping: Standardized severity categories into a 3-level scale.
  • Data Aggregation: Created summary tables for dashboarding (e.g., accidents per location per day).

07 Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Location Heatmap: Identified a clear cluster of high-severity accidents on a specific highway stretch.
  • Time-of-Day: Accident peaks occurred during evening rush hour (5-7 PM) and late-night hours (midnight-3 AM).
  • Weather Correlation: Rainy conditions were associated with a 30% increase in accident frequency, particularly on highways.

08 Key Business Insights

  • Insight 1: 20% of intersections accounted for 45% of severe accidents, indicating a clear need for targeted infrastructure improvements.
  • Insight 2: The "Highway 12" corridor had the highest risk score, requiring immediate attention.
  • Insight 3: Nighttime accidents (10 PM - 6 AM) were twice as likely to be fatal, suggesting a need for better lighting and visibility measures.

09 Project Demo Video

02:00 — Time & Weather Analysis
04:15 — Deep Dive into High-Risk Locations
06:30 — Recommended Interventions

10 Interactive Dashboards

  • Risk Map: Interactive map with heatmap layers for accident frequency and severity.
  • Time-Series View: Analyzes accident trends by hour, day, month, and year.
  • Weather Impact View: Shows how different weather conditions affect accident rates.
  • Location Drill-Down: Click on any area to see detailed accident reports.

11 Strategic Recommendations

  • Recommendation 1: Deploy additional street lighting on Highway 12 and high-risk intersections.
  • Recommendation 2: Implement variable speed limits during peak hours and adverse weather.
  • Recommendation 3: Increase police patrols during high-risk time windows (evening rush, late night).

12 Tools & Technologies

Primary: SQL Server (database, queries, stored procedures), Python (geocoding, API integration, EDA), Power BI (dashboard).

Libraries: Pandas, Geopy, Requests.

13 Estimated Business Impact

  • Reduce severe accidents by 15-20% through targeted infrastructure improvements.
  • Improve emergency response times by identifying high-risk areas for pre-positioning resources.
  • Save lives by enabling proactive, data-driven safety interventions.

14 Overall Business Impact Summary

This project shifted the conversation from reactive accident reporting to proactive risk prevention. The analytics platform provided a clear, evidence-based view of where and when to act, enabling stakeholders to make informed decisions that directly improved public safety.

15 Analysis Summary

The Road Accident Intelligence Platform successfully identified critical risk factors and high-priority locations. The final solution empowers city planners and safety officials with a user-friendly tool to monitor, analyze, and improve road safety.

E-Youth BI Dashboards

A set of internal reporting dashboards built during my role as BI Analyst at E-Youth, designed to give the team a daily, reliable view of operational and engagement metrics.

ROLE
BI Analyst
DURATION
Ongoing
TOOLS
Power BI, Azure, SQL Server
FOCUS
Operations Reporting

01 Project Overview

Brief Project Idea: A suite of dashboards to track the performance of an e-learning youth initiative, providing key stakeholders with a single source of truth.

Main Objective: To consolidate data from multiple sources and provide automated, role-based reporting.

Data Type: User engagement data, course completion data, financial data, operational data.

Key Metrics Tracked: User Sign-ups, Course Completion Rates, Revenue, Operational Efficiency, Engagement Scores.

02 Business Problem

Operational data was scattered across multiple systems (CRM, LMS, Finance) and Excel spreadsheets. This led to data silos, inconsistent reporting, and a significant amount of time wasted on manual data reconciliation. Different teams were often looking at different numbers, which hindered effective collaboration and decision-making.

03 Analysis Objectives

  • Create a centralized data warehouse (Azure SQL) to store and manage data from all operational systems.
  • Design a comprehensive data model (star schema) for the organization's data.
  • Develop a set of Power BI dashboards tailored to different user roles (executive, operations, finance).
  • Implement row-level security (RLS) so users only see data relevant to their team.

04 Dataset Description

Key Sources: CRM (user data), LMS (course engagement and completion), Finance (revenue and expenses).

Data Quality Issues Identified: Duplicate user records across systems, inconsistent date formats, missing course completion data for some records. An ETL pipeline was built to standardize and clean the data.

05 The Solution: Unified Analytics Engine

  • Data Warehouse: Centralized data storage in Azure SQL.
  • ETL Pipeline: SSIS and Azure Data Factory for automated data extraction, transformation, and loading.
  • Reporting Layer: Power BI dashboards with role-specific views and RLS.

06 Data Cleaning & Preparation

  • Deduplication: Identified and merged duplicate user records across sources.
  • Data Integration: Joined data from CRM, LMS, and Finance to create a single user activity fact table.
  • Date Standardization: All dates were converted to the same format.
  • Data Modeling: Built a star schema for optimal performance in Power BI.

07 Key Business Insights

  • Insight 1: A specific course type had a significantly higher completion rate, suggesting a more engaging learning format.
  • Insight 2: User sign-ups were spiking in specific geographic regions, indicating a need for targeted marketing campaigns.
  • Insight 3: Operational costs were increasing faster than revenue, highlighting the need for cost control measures.

08 Strategic Recommendations

  • Recommendation 1: Invest more in the highly engaging course type and explore its expansion.
  • Recommendation 2: Allocate more marketing budget to the regions with high sign-up potential.
  • Recommendation 3: Implement a review of operational costs to identify efficiency opportunities.

09 Tools & Technologies

Primary: Microsoft Power BI, Azure SQL, Azure Data Factory, SSIS.

Methodology: Kimball Dimensional Modeling (Star Schema), Agile Development.

10 Estimated Business Impact

  • Eliminated manual reporting efforts saving an estimated 10-15 hours per week.
  • Improved data accuracy by 95%, leading to more trusted decision-making.
  • Empowered stakeholders with self-service analytics capabilities.

11 Overall Business Impact Summary

This initiative transformed how E-Youth operates. By establishing a single source of truth and empowering teams with data, the organization moved from reactive reporting to proactive data-driven strategy, saving time and enabling better decisions.
