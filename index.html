Road Accident Analysis

Project Description

This project analyzed road accident data to identify patterns, high-risk locations, and contributing factors. The analysis helped transportation authorities develop targeted safety interventions and allocate resources more effectively.

By examining temporal patterns, weather conditions, and road characteristics, the project provided actionable insights that contributed to a 15% reduction in accidents in targeted areas.

Project Video

Click to play project walkthrough

Dashboard Views

Key Findings

  • 25% of accidents occurred between 3 PM and 6 PM, during evening rush hour
  • Intersections without traffic signals had 3x more accidents than those with signals
  • Rainy conditions increased accident likelihood by 40% compared to dry conditions
  • 60% of severe accidents involved speeding as a contributing factor
  • 3 specific highway segments accounted for 18% of all accidents in the dataset

Recommendations

  • Install traffic signals at high-risk intersections identified in the analysis
  • Increase police patrols during high-risk time periods (3 PM - 6 PM)
  • Implement speed cameras on highway segments with high accident rates
  • Improve road signage and lighting in areas with frequent accidents during rainy conditions
  • Launch public awareness campaigns targeting speeding during evening commute hours
